NVIDIA reported its financial results for the first quarter of fiscal 2024, ending April 30, 2023. Here are the key highlights:

- **Quarterly Revenue**: $7.19 billion, which is a 19% increase from the previous quarter but a 13% decrease compared to the same quarter last year.
- **Data Center Revenue**: Achieved a record of $4.28 billion.
- **Earnings**:
- GAAP earnings per diluted share: $0.82, up 28% year-over-year and 44% quarter-over-quarter.
- Non-GAAP earnings per diluted share: $1.09, down 20% year-over-year but up 24% quarter-over-quarter.
- **Gross Margin**:
- GAAP: 64.6%, a slight increase from the previous quarter but a decrease from the same quarter last year.
- Non-GAAP: 66.8%, also showing a slight increase from the previous quarter but a decrease year-over-year.
- **Operating Expenses**: Decreased to $2.508 billion, down 3% from the previous quarter and down 30% from the same quarter last year.
- **Net Income**: $2.043 billion, up 44% from the previous quarter and 26% from the same quarter last year.
- **Shareholder Returns**: NVIDIA returned $99 million in cash dividends during the quarter and announced a quarterly cash dividend of $0.04 per share to be paid on June 30, 2023.

**Outlook for Q2 Fiscal 2024**: NVIDIA anticipates revenue of $11.00 billion for the second quarter.

Overall, the company is experiencing significant growth in its data center business and is preparing to meet increasing demand for its products related to accelerated computing and generative AI.
